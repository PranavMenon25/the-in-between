The InBetween

"Never trust the light, sugar. Never."

Ava Benson died a second before she knew it. Now she walks a world an inch from the living — where memory dissolves, touch burns, and the only way out is the one thing no one wants to give.

The Story

Worlds apart, an inch of air between them.

Ava follows her grieving mother to the pond at the centre of town, close enough to see her tears — and unable to be seen, smelled, or touched. This is the in-between: the waiting room of the dead, where souls linger until the lights come for them.

Guided by a fifty-pound chihuahua who can smell death, warned by an ancient voice called Ol' Nellie, and hunted by a man with blue eyes she cannot remember killing, Ava pieces her final days back together — one dissolving memory at a time.

Because in the in-between, the past doesn't stay buried. It returns exactly when it means business. And somewhere in it waits Dexter — the boy who loved her, the boy who paid for it, and the only soul whose forgiveness can end the loop.

They were separated by an inch of air, but in actuality they were worlds apart. Chapter One — Ava in the In Between

Field Notes from the Dead

The rules of the in-between.

On touch

The living cannot be held.

Reach across and the world pushes back — with a force stronger than anything you can exert, and a burn that leaves black blood behind. The dead can touch only each other.

On memory

The mind unravels here.

Memories churn, split, and fuse until time loses meaning. You forget your favourite taste, your best friend's face — and, eventually, how you died. They return only when they mean business.

On release

Forgiveness opens the door.

When someone truly forgives — and is truly forgiven — something loosens. That's when the lights come. They look like mercy. They do not forgive.

On the lights

Never trust them.

They arrive without warning and leave without apology, taking whoever is ready — mid-sentence, mid-love, mid-life. Some souls wait a thousand years. Some are taken in ten seconds.

The Souls

Everyone here is waiting for something.

The Good Daughter

Ava Benson

She helps the newly dead cross over, holds her mother back from the edge, and cannot remember the worst thing she ever did. The lights come for everyone except her.

The Quiet One

Dexter

He couldn't stay quiet for the length of a sneeze — until his last words were silence. A joke machine with the door always open, and no one ever asked what it cost him. He has unfinished business.

The Man with Blue Eyes

Evon

Everyone loses themselves in his eyes; that's the trick. He knows how to keep what he never wanted, and how to mend a crack with cement. He wants the lights — and he thinks Ava can give them to him.

The Mother

Claire Benson

She whispers to a pond in a memorial park, hoping her daughter is listening. She once stood over a monster like she owned the space. No one hurts her daughter. No one.

The One Who Smells Death

Bessie

A chihuahua who found Ava the moment she fell into the in-between, and the only thing that kept her sane. She leads Ava everywhere she needs to go — even to the truth.

The Voice

Ol' Nellie

Ancient, raspy, and everywhere at once. No one remembers her face; everyone remembers her warning. She speaks through the newly dead like a recording no one made. Who is she, really?
